Pitch Duel

Three-Stage Prediction Pipeline

Pitch Duel is a competitive AI simulator that answers one question for every pitcher: "How does my pitch hold up against specific hitters?"

The idea started from the 2026 WBC, watching international pitchers (many of them are the best in their home leagues) getting exposed by MLB hitters with low confidence. This was not because they weren't talented enough. I believe it was because they had never had the chance to train against them. I want this model to be a tool for pitchers everywhere to get that experience — to step onto the mound knowing exactly how their best stuff plays against the different hitters in the world, and to use that knowledge to prepare better and compete at a higher level.

How it works
The model is trained on over a decade of MLB Statcast pitch data and builds a behavioral profile for each named hitter — how they attack specific zones, which pitch families they struggle with, how their swing decisions shift by count. Input a pitch, pick a hitter, and the three-stage model predicts the probability the hitter swings, makes contact, and makes hard contact — along with a continuous expected damage estimate for the quality of contact if it's made. Any pitcher anywhere in the world can now train against an AI version of the best hitters on the planet.
This demo vs. the full model
This demo uses manual pitch input — you enter velocity, spin, movement, and location by hand. The production model is designed to connect directly to pitch tracking hardware: Trackman Rapsodo Hawk-Eye
In a live session, every pitch off the mound feeds into the model in real time — no manual entry, no delay. Select your target hitter before the session starts and get pitch-by-pitch feedback as you throw. The manual demo below lets you explore the model's output with any pitch profile you want to test.
